My Teaching Approach:

I'm passionate about working with students who have developed a strained relationship with math. My goal throughout our meetings is to press back against the math fear and restore student confidence. In practice, this means I emphasize a student's goals as hard work and personal improvement. You can expect that I bring my enthusiastic love of mathematics to the meetings, which is often contagious and engaging. You can also expect that I teach to different learning styles and meet students at whatever level they are, strongly encouraging questions as they come up.

As meetings continue, I will develop a sense of the student's needs, taking into account their personal concerns as well as things they might not know to fix. This includes providing the math background they're missing, updating study strategies that would work better for their learning style, and bringing their attention to the sections they perform inconsistently on to prioritize time spent studying. When students excel at a particular topic, I will sprinkle in advanced questions on the subject so they are engaged and able to grow as a student, and we'll still get through required materials.

It's an ambitious dream, but I want to bring joy back into mathematics!

Considerations when Scheduling

While remote tutoring is convenient, you should not request remote meetings unless you have access to a tablet and have the ability to screenshare! Meetings can happen any day of the week, including weekends within the 10AM-5PM EST window. College students requesting in-person meetings should plan to meet in a university study space. Students should feel welcome to email or text me with math emergencies, but depending on the size of the question, the frequency of emails, or the time the email is sent, I may have a delayed reply or suggest a brief meeting in place of a typed answer.
